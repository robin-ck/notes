# Table 18: Summary of Challenges, Gaps, and Proposed Future Directions

> Source: Ray, S. K. (2025). Integrating Geographic Information System, Artificial Intelligence, and Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis: A Comprehensive Review for Sustainable Urban Settlement Planning. *Applied Spatial Analysis and Policy*, 18, 155. https://doi.org/10.1007/s12061-025-09762-3

| Category | Key Issues | Proposed Future Directions |
|---|---|---|
| **Technical & Operational** | - Platform interoperability hurdles <br> - High computational demands <br> - Skill gaps in planning agencies | - Develop standardized APIs and data formats <br> - Leverage cloud/GPU resources <br> - Invest in capacity-building programs |
| **Data & Infrastructure** | - Data scarcity and fragmentation <br> - Inconsistent resolutions and formats <br> - Lack of centralized repositories | - Mandate open data policies <br> - Harmonize data schemas <br> - Build urban data platforms |
| **Governance, Ethics & Inclusion** | - Centralized decision-making <br> - Algorithmic bias and opacity <br> - Limited stakeholder engagement | - Adopt ethical AI frameworks <br> - Enforce transparency and participatory protocols <br> - Expand civil-society partnerships |
| **Research & Methodological Gaps** | - Real-time integration lacking <br> - Static MCDA weightings <br> - Limited cross-scale interoperability | - Develop adaptive MCDA models <br> - Create streaming data pipelines <br> - Research multi-scale frameworks |
| **Innovation & Policy Alignment** | - Slow policy adoption <br> - Absence of urban tech sandboxes | - Establish policy-tech testbeds <br> - Align regulations with emerging tech standards |
