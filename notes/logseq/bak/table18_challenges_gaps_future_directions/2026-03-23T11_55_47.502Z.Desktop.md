# Table 18: Summary of Challenges, Gaps, and Proposed Future Directions

> Source: Ray, S. K. (2025). Integrating Geographic Information System, Artificial Intelligence, and Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis: A Comprehensive Review for Sustainable Urban Settlement Planning. *Applied Spatial Analysis and Policy*, 18, 155. https://doi.org/10.1007/s12061-025-09762-3

## Technical & Operational

| Key Issues | Proposed Future Directions |
|---|---|
| Platform interoperability hurdles | Develop standardized APIs and data formats |
| High computational demands | Leverage cloud/GPU resources |
| Skill gaps in planning agencies | Invest in capacity-building programs |

## Data & Infrastructure

| Key Issues | Proposed Future Directions |
|---|---|
| Data scarcity and fragmentation | Mandate open data policies |
| Inconsistent resolutions and formats | Harmonize data schemas |
| Lack of centralized repositories | Build urban data platforms |

## Governance, Ethics & Inclusion

| Key Issues | Proposed Future Directions |
|---|---|
| Centralized decision-making | Adopt ethical AI frameworks |
| Algorithmic bias and opacity | Enforce transparency and participatory protocols |
| Limited stakeholder engagement | Expand civil-society partnerships |

## Research & Methodological Gaps

| Key Issues | Proposed Future Directions |
|---|---|
| Real-time integration lacking | Develop adaptive MCDA models |
| Static MCDA weightings | Create streaming data pipelines |
| Limited cross-scale interoperability | Research multi-scale frameworks |

## Innovation & Policy Alignment

| Key Issues | Proposed Future Directions |
|---|---|
| Slow policy adoption | Establish policy-tech testbeds |
| Absence of urban tech sandboxes | Align regulations with emerging tech standards |
