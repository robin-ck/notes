topic:: BGI
- **Reduced Flood Risk**
	- BGI restores natural environmental features, alleviates floods by delaying flood flows, reducing runoff volume, and promoting rainfall infiltration
- **Reduced and Delayed Stormwater Runoff Volumes**
	- Vegetation and soils retain and absorb rainfall; increased pervious cover raises infiltration rates, reducing stormwater entering sewer systems
- **Stormwater Pollutant Reductions**
	- BGI infiltrates runoff near its source; vegetation and microbes naturally filter and break down most pollutants
- **Reduced Sewer Overflow Events**
	- Natural retention and infiltration limits frequency of sewer overflows by reducing runoff volumes and delaying discharge
- **Improved Waterway Quality**
	- Filters, biologically or chemically degrades pollutants; reduces erosion, sedimentation, and pollutant concentrations in rivers, lakes, and streams
- **Enhanced Water Conservation**
	- Rainwater harvesting and drought-tolerant landscaping reduce potable and recycled water demand; mitigates water scarcity from population growth and climate change
- **Increased Groundwater Recharge**
	- Swales and rain gardens reduce impervious surfaces, allowing rainwater infiltration to replenish aquifers and sustain baseflow in rivers and streams
- **Improved Air Quality and Lower Greenhouse Gas Emissions**
	- Trees absorb gaseous pollutants, intercept particulates, and lower temperatures by transpiration, reducing ground-level ozone (smog)
	- BGI reduces wastewater volumes requiring treatment, cutting greenhouse gas emissions
- **Reduced Urban Heat Island Effects**
	- Green space and vegetation counteract heat-absorbing pavements and buildings; green roofs and trees lower air conditioning demand and power plant emissions
- **Improved Habitats**
	- Parks, urban forests, wetlands, and vegetated swales provide wildlife habitat; green corridors conserve and promote biodiversity
- **Carbon Sequestration**
	- BGI soils and vegetation capture CO2 via photosynthesis (e.g. Portland, Oregon estimates ~8,800 metric tonnes CO2 sequestered annually by BGI)
