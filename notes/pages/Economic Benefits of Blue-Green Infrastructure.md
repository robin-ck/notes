- **Deferring or Replacing Costly Grey Infrastructure**
	- BGI can defer or replace costly grey infrastructure (e.g. sewage expansions, deep tunnels)
	- More flexible cash flows; lower built capital, land, operational, repair, and replacement costs
- **Reduced Water Treatment Costs**
	- Rainfall filtered and treated naturally, reducing need for chemical and energy-intensive purification
- **Lower Landscape Maintenance Costs**
	- Rainwater harvesting systems and drought-resistant plants reduce irrigation and maintenance costs
- **Increased Groundwater Resources**
	- Higher groundwater recharge avoids increased pumping costs from declining groundwater levels
- **Reduced Water Imports**
	- BGI reduces landscape irrigation demand, lowering reliance on costly long-distance water imports
- **Reduced Energy Costs**
	- Green roofs provide insulation/shade; rain gardens raise groundwater reducing pumping energy; rainwater harvesting reduces potable water treatment energy
- **Enhanced Ecosystem Service Values**
	- Water filtration, air filtration, carbon storage, nutrient cycling, recreation, and food — often undervalued as they are not fully monetised
- **Increased Employment**
	- Direct jobs in construction, maintenance, and management; indirect jobs through BGI-linked tourism
- **Increased Investment**
	- Improves regional image, attracting high-value industries, start-ups, entrepreneurs, and workers
- **Increased Land and Property Values**
	- BGI in key urban locations raises nearby property values, attracts retail and leisure spending, and stimulates economic growth
