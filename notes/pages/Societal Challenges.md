- Source: Cohen-Shacham et al. (2016), Chapter 2
- NbS are designed to address a range of **major societal challenges** — see [[Nature-based Solutions]]
- ## Water Security
	- ![image.png](../assets/image_1772813776586_0.png)
	- Built infrastructure alone is **insufficient** for future water security given climate change
	- ~4 billion people (60% of world population) live in near-permanent **water stress**
	- NbS harness water-related services of **natural infrastructure** (forests, wetlands, floodplains)
	- Key insight: "**water for nature and nature for water**" — nature is both source of water and a water user
	- Both built and natural infrastructure are needed — they are **complementary**
	- Addressed under **SDG 6** (sustainable water management, integrated water resource management) ) [[Sustainable Development Goals]]
- ## Food Security
	- ![image.png](../assets/image_1772813808903_0.png)
	- Food security = availability, accessibility, safety, local appropriateness, and reliability of food
	- >795 million people undernourished globally (FAO, 2015)
	- "Technical fixes" to food production are **insufficient** on their own — solutions must be multi-faceted
	- NbS entry points include:
		- Protecting **wild genetic resources** (animal and plant)
		- Managing **wild species** (especially fish)
		- Providing **irrigation water**
		- Restoration, conservation and management of ecosystems for food stability during disasters or climate shocks
		- Specific approaches: forest landscape restoration, addressing land tenure, protecting plant resources from pests
	- Addressed under **SDG 2** (zero hunger) [[Sustainable Development Goals]]
- ## Human Health
	- ![image.png](../assets/image_1772813827354_0.png)
	- Ecosystems, climate and biodiversity are recognised **determinants of human health, well-being and social cohesion**
	- Benefits of green space include:
		- **Heat regulation and noise abatement**
		- Promotion of **physical activity**
		- Enhanced **social interaction, inclusion and cohesion**
		- Opportunities for **spiritual well-being**
	- Forests and coral reefs also provide a source of **medicines and pharmaceutical products**
	- Requires **broader stakeholder collaboration** and integration across policy domains
	- Addressed under **SDG 3, SDG 11, SDG 13** ) [[Sustainable Development Goals]]
- ## Disaster Risk Reduction
	- ![image.png](../assets/image_1772813838836_0.png)
	- Ecosystems can reduce physical exposure to natural hazards by serving as **protective barriers or buffers**
	- Evidence: US$1 invested in Folkestone Marine National Park (Barbados) avoids US$20 million in annual hurricane damages
	- Without mangroves, rice crops take **3x longer** to recover from salt intrusion after coastal storms (India)
	- These insights led to the development of the **Eco-DRR approach** → [[Categories of NbS]]
	- Recognition growing in global frameworks: CBD (2014), **Sendai Framework** (2015), Ramsar Convention (2015)
	- Scaling up requires facilitated dialogues between scientists, policy makers and conservation/disaster management practitioners
	- Addressed under **SDG 11** (resilient cities) and **SDG 13** (climate action); also contributes to SDGs 1, 2, 3, 6, 15 ) [[Sustainable Development Goals]]
- ## Climate Change
	- ![image.png](../assets/image_1772813852169_0.png)
	- NbS contribute to both **mitigation and adaptation**
	- **Ecosystem-based Mitigation (EbM):**
		- Deforestation and forest degradation release ~4.4 Gt CO2/year (~12% of anthropogenic emissions)
		- Land sector (AFOLU) contributes ~24% of annual global anthropogenic emissions (IPCC, 2014)
		- Restoring 350 million ha of degraded/deforested landscapes by 2030 can sequester **1-3 billion tonnes CO2e/year** and generate ~US$170 billion/year in other ecosystem service benefits
	- **Natural carbon sinks:**
		- ~60% of cumulative anthropogenic GHG emissions since pre-industrial era stored on land or in the ocean
		- Conservation, restoration and sustainable management of forests, wetlands and oceans is critical for the **carbon cycle**
	- **Ecosystem-based Adaptation (EbA):** [Categories of NbS](#Ecosystem-based Adaptation (EbA))
		- Natural infrastructure can **complement and enhance** physical infrastructure (sea walls, dykes)
	- No long-term climate solution can succeed **without** fully drawing on NbS
	- Addressed under **SDG 13** ) [[Sustainable Development Goals]]
