## Natural Capital and NbS (Section 2.6)
	- **Natural capital** = the finite stock of environmental assets (water, land, air, species, minerals) producing ecosystem goods and services
	- **Natural Capital Approaches (NCAP)** aim to make the value of nature **visible in decision making**
	- Natural capital is **complementary** to manufactured, financial, human and social capital
	- Key debate: degree to which natural capital can be **substituted** by manufactured capital
		- Dominant economic model assumes substitution is possible
		- Concept of **critical natural capital** — some natural capital is essential and has no substitutes (Ekins, 2003)
		- Echoed by the **planetary boundaries** framework (Rockstrom et al., 2009)
	- Example: natural capital can substitute for manufactured capital (mangroves vs. seawalls for coastal defence)
